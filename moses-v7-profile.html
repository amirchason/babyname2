Religious Significance

Moses the Prophet and Lawgiver

Religions: Judaism, Christianity, Islam

Moses led the Israelites out of Egyptian slavery in the Exodus, received the Ten Commandments and Torah from God on Mount Sinai, and guided the Hebrew people through 40 years in the wilderness toward the Promised Land.

Key Stories:
  • Found in a basket on the Nile River as a baby
  • The burning bush encounter where God revealed His name
  • Confronting Pharaoh and the ten plagues of Egypt
  • Parting the Red Sea to escape Egyptian armies
  • Receiving the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai
  • Leading the Israelites for 40 years in the wilderness
  • Viewing the Promised Land from Mount Nebo before his death

Spiritual Meaning: The name embodies themes of divine calling, liberation from oppression, covenant with God, and faithful obedience despite challenges.
