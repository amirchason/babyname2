The name Giana has been used in various cultures, particularly within Italian and Christian contexts. It reflects a connection to grace and divine favor, which resonates in many societies where religious influence is strong.

As of 2024-2025, the name Giana is experiencing a resurgence in popularity, particularly in the United States and Italy, favored by parents seeking a unique yet traditional name.

Saint Gianna Beretta Molla

Religions: Christianity

Saint Gianna Beretta Molla was an Italian pediatrician and a canonized saint known for her selfless sacrifice during childbirth, embodying the virtues of motherhood and grace.

Key Stories:
  • Gianna chose to prioritize her unborn child over her own health during pregnancy.
  • She was canonized by Pope John Paul II in 2004.
  • Gianna's feast day is celebrated on April 28.

Spiritual Meaning: Her life is often viewed as a testament to selflessness and maternal love.
