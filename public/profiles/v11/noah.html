The Story Behind the Name

Noah has its roots anchored firmly in the Hebrew language, where it is derived from the word 'Noach', meaning 'rest' or 'comfort'. This meaning resonates deeply when you consider the story of Noah from the Hebrew Bible—a man who, through faith and perseverance, became a symbol of solace and survival during a time of great upheaval. The name suggests a sense of peace and refuge, qualities that have helped it endure the passage of time and transcend cultural boundaries.

Over the centuries, Noah has maintained its original charm while adapting to the diverse cultural landscapes it has traveled through.
