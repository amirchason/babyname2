The Story Behind the Name

Delving into the etymology of Charlotte is like unfolding the layers of a rich tapestry woven with history and meaning. Originating from the French word 'Charles,' which translates to 'free man,' Charlotte is its feminine counterpart, meaning 'free woman.' This name has journeyed through centuries and across continents, retaining its regal elegance and emblematic spirit of freedom.

Charlotte's journey from being a derivative of Charles to becoming a standalone feminine name is a fascinating evolution. In French aristocracy, the name Charlotte was often given to daughters to symbolize strength and independence, traits that were esteemed even in times when women's roles were more confined. Today, Charlotte has evolved beyond its royal roots to become a beloved name cherished for its sweet sophistication and enduring grace.
